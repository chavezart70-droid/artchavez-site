Generally: two years of tax returns and W-2s, 30 days of pay stubs, two months of bank statements, a government-issued ID, and details on any current debts. Self-employed borrowers will also need a profit and loss statement. I give you a complete checklist tailored to your situation from the start.
It depends on how far down and what loan program you currently have. Some government-backed programs allow refinancing with limited equity. I run the numbers and give you an honest picture of whether refinancing makes financial sense before you commit to anything.
A cash-out refinance replaces your existing mortgage with a larger loan and gives you the difference in cash. It can make sense for home improvements, paying off debt, or investing, but only when the rate, term, and long-term cost actually work in your favor. I do a full cost-benefit analysis before recommending it.
